KID LESSONS

Tennis basics for kids: grip, stance, swing. Fun drills, teamwork, and games make learning enjoyable and interactive.

"Tennis is all about laughter, good vibes, and a wicked backhand. So, let's smack some smiles onto that court and enjoy every swing!"
Dhaka Oke, Tennis Coach

Overview: Children have a great capacity to learn and typically pick up new skills quickly. It is therefore advantageous to learn to play tennis at a young age, as they will grow up to have a solid foundation in a sport that can be enjoyed for life.

Our coaches will introduce tennis in a fun and engaging way. We will build your kid’s interest in tennis through meaningful activities that train hand-eye coordination, balance, and agility. Your child will feel a sense of pride from being able to hit his or her first ball over the net!

WEEK 1: INTRODUCTION

  • In the first 15 minutes, kids will be introduced to the coach and the tennis environment. They will explore rackets, balls, and the court layout to satisfy their curiosity. The coach will focus on familiarity rather than deep mechanics.
  • From 15 to 35 minutes, grip types will be introduced, and drills like balancing and bouncing the ball on the racket enhance control and understanding. The last 20 minutes involve progressing through various drills as kids grasp each skill.

WEEK 2: FOOTWORK

  • In the second lesson, the focus is on fundamental tennis mechanics. The initial 15 minutes involve supervised static stretches and light jogging to prepare. Dynamic warm-ups, tossing tennis balls over the net, help kids understand ball bounces and positioning.
  • From 15 to 35 minutes, the coach refreshes grip knowledge and introduces forehand and backhand techniques in four steps. Drills incorporate ball feeding to practice the newly learned techniques.
  • The next 20 minutes include games like 'Catch' to enhance bounce and trajectory understanding. The lesson concludes with a 5-minute warm-down, followed by a debrief with both the child and their caregiver, addressing any questions or reviewing the day's learning.

WEEK 3: BASIC STROKE

  • In the third lesson, kids focus on fundamental groundstroke techniques, honing hand-eye coordination and ball understanding. The session begins with a supervised 15-minute static stretch and light jogging. Dynamic warm-ups involve catching coach-fed tennis balls, progressively increasing in difficulty to familiarize students with movement, racket control, and swing techniques.
  • From 15 to 35 minutes, coaches build on previous drills, incorporating shadow swings and tennis balls with increasing pace. Shadow swings aid muscle memory development and error identification. Hand-fed tennis balls simulate realistic groundstroke scenarios, gradually increasing in speed.
  • The remaining time (35-60 mins) includes bounce and trajectory understanding games, such as modified 'Catch.' The session concludes with a warm-down, stretching activated muscle groups, and a debrief with the child and caregiver to review the day's lessons.

WEEK 4: NET PLAY

  • In the fourth lesson, net-play techniques, including volleys and smashes, are introduced. The initial 15 minutes involve supervised static stretches and light jogging. Dynamic warm-ups include catching coach-fed tennis balls, gradually increasing in difficulty.
  • From 15 to 35 minutes, coaches explain and demonstrate proper volley and smash techniques, emphasizing footwork, 'ready position,' and fundamental movements. Drills focus on forehand and backhand volleys, along with step-by-step smash instructions.
  • Dynamic drills from 35 to 55 minutes combine net-play techniques, introducing combinations like forehand volley-backhand volley-smash. Games like 'Lava Ball' enhance footwork, positioning, and ball trajectory understanding.
  • The lesson concludes with a 5-minute warm-down, stretching activated muscle groups, and a debrief with the child and caregiver to review the day's learnings.
