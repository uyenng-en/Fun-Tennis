Our Program

Overview: Tailored for adult players of varied skill levels, tennis coaching offers dynamic sessions to enhance proficiency and enjoyment. Coaches employ a comprehensive approach, combining on-court drills, strategic discussions, and match simulations to refine techniques and deepen players' understanding of the game. Structured programs cater to beginners, intermediate, and advanced levels, covering fundamental skills for beginners, strategic play for intermediates, and advanced techniques for experienced players.

The coaching environment fosters a supportive community, where players not only develop their game but also forge lasting connections and a genuine love for the sport.

WEEK 1: INTRODUCTION

  • In your initial 10-15 minutes, grasp continental and eastern grips, stance, and basic tennis rules. Proceed to 10-15 minutes of shadow swings, emphasizing posture and footwork. Practice with tossed balls at the service line for 10-15 minutes to assess your competency.
  • For beginners, spend time tossing balls without rackets to understand ball trajectory, focusing on spin and bounce. Move on to racket drills, addressing footwork, swing, and distance from the ball—taking 30-45 minutes.
  • By the lesson's end, gain ball sense, understand forehand and backhand mechanics, and learn self-correction. Your foundation is set for tennis proficiency.

WEEK 2-3: CONTROLS

  • Lesson two focuses on groundstroke control, emphasizing racket-face influence, early/late contact, and the importance of topspin. The 5-minute warm-up includes forehand and backhand ball-tossing.
  • A 15-minute drill at the service-line advances to balls tossed away, requiring side steps and proper footwork. Progress to a 20-minute drill with faster balls, first at the service-line, then the baseline. Spend 20 minutes consciously generating topspin, understanding its effects on bounce and trajectory.
  • Conclude with 20 minutes of mini-tennis, applying topspin. While not yet sustaining rallies, you gain shot control. Practice is encouraged for improved ball sense.

WEEK 4-5: VOLLEY

  • Learn the volley with a 5-10 minute mini-tennis warm-up or service-line drills if needed. Master forehand and backhand volleys with proper posture, footwork, and a shortened backswing.
  • Footwork: Step into the ball, emphasizing a split-step. Posture: Use the continental grip, keep the racket up, and be ready for both volleys. Backswing: Practice starting in a volley position to punch the ball, eliminating unnecessary backswing.
  • Conclude with a net approach drill. After 20-30 minutes, gain confidence in volleying, holding rallies at the service-line or even short baseline rallies. Understand the game-changing impact of volleys and practice for improved control in longer rallies.

WEEK 5-6: MATCH-PLAY

  • In lesson four, focus on serving and match basics. Warm up with a 5-10 minute mini-tennis session, assessing posture, groundstrokes, and footwork.
  • Learn the serve in 30-40 minutes, breaking it into toss, stance, racket-swing, and contact-point. Practice tossing near a cone, adopting an open stance, and understanding the wrist and elbow dynamics.
  • Engage in 10-15 minutes of match-play, applying what you've learned. By the lesson's end, achieve some successful serves, hold a decent full-court rally, and grasp scoring. Although challenging, solo practice and video analysis can aid service improvement.
  • After these four lessons, you will gain a basic understanding of tennis, exhibiting control, consistency, and the ability to self-correct.

WEEK 6-9: PERSONAL PLAYSTYLE

  • Lesson five focuses on mastering the slice, a versatile tool enhancing control, accuracy, and spin in your game. Learn back-spin and side-spin effects through mini-tennis at the service-line with a continental grip.
  • Practice control from the baseline, alternating down-the-line and cross-court slices. Enhance accuracy by hitting into specific quadrants using cones. Develop deliberate spin, adjusting depth for defensive slices, dropping with backspin, and using side-spin to widen angles.
  • This comprehensive slice training takes 45-60 minutes. By the lesson's end, master the slice's nuances, react effectively to diverse shots, and strategically apply slices in matches for an improved and dynamic game.

WEEK 9-12: ADVANCED TACTICS

  • In the final phase of the 12-week plan, explore advanced techniques, gameplay tactics, and service variations at your own pace. Delve into transitional strokes like the half-volley and inside-out groundstrokes, focusing on footwork and control in mini-tennis drills. The session may last 60-90 minutes.
  • Evaluate risk and reward through risk assessments, understanding shot importance, potential, and opponent considerations, enhancing your strategic approach over 60-90 minutes.
  • Advance your service skills with flat, slice, and kick serves, adding variety and unpredictability to your game. Develop each serve's unique mechanics with your coach's guidance.
  • Gameplay tactics, including serve-and-volley, baseline grinding, and opportunistic play, will be explored over 60-90 minutes. These strategies emphasize mental acuity, allowing you to adapt dynamically during matches.
  • By the end, you should possess a well-rounded game, capable of strategic play, varied serves, and advanced strokes. Your newfound confidence, adaptability, and technical knowledge empower you to self-correct and continually improve your tennis skills.
